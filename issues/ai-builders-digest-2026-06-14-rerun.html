English

Levie on why the model routing layer will increase in value substantially. Three reasons: (1) Cost optimization—frontier for planning/review, cheaper model for bulk work. (2) Capability maximization—different models excel at tool use, coding, knowledge work. (3) Risk mitigation—the Fable situation proves you need flexibility to deploy across providers. 'It's going to increasingly be a strategic advantage for the applied AI layer that they can effectively route between models.' 209 likes.

The layer that can route to the best AI model for the particular job is going to increase in value substantially. Cost optimization: frontier intelligence for some tasks, something far cheaper for others. Capability maximization: some better at tool use, others better at coding. Risk mitigation: the Fable situation is somewhat of a black swan, but it's possible we're heading toward a regulatory environment where governments may restrict models at different times.
